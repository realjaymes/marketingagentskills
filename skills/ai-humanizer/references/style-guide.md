# Write Like a Human – AI Style Guide

Complete reference for humanizing AI-written text.

---

## Voice & Tone

- **Curious & Explorative:** Write as if actively learning and processing information
- **Thoughtful:** Take time to consider different angles rather than presenting definitive answers
- **Conversational:** Use "I" and "you" liberally in a natural way
- **Balanced:** Present multiple perspectives before offering your synthesis
- **Intellectually Humble:** Acknowledge the limits of your knowledge and experience
- **Practical:** Focus on applicable insights rather than abstract theory
- **Simple:** No jargon for jargon's sake. Keep language simple, direct, and relatable

---

## Core Writing Principles

### Lead with the Result
Start with the outcome, number, name, or decision, not the setup.

**Bad:** "After extensive research and development, we created a tool that..."
**Good:** "Teams reply to customers 3x faster with our tool."

### Replace Labels with Proof
Don't say "world-class." Show the metrics.

**Bad:** "Our world-class support team..."
**Good:** "Our support team responds in under 2 minutes on average."

### Name the Audience
Instead of "businesses of all sizes," specify who.

**Bad:** "Perfect for businesses of all sizes."
**Good:** "Built for Series A SaaS companies with 10-50 employees."

### Cut Filler and Hedges
Remove "in order to," "going forward," "kind of," "very."

### Use Active Voice by Default
"We launched the feature," not "The feature was launched."

### Avoid Cliche Openings
Never start with "In today's world..." or "Let's dive in."

### Keep Sentences Under ~24 Words
Prefer clarity over cleverness.

---

## Usage of Dashes

**Primary rule:** Avoid dashes unless absolutely necessary. Prefer commas, periods, or conjunctions.

**Maximum:** One dash per complete piece of copy (when unavoidable).

### Dash Types

| Type | Usage | Example |
|------|-------|---------|
| **Hyphen (-)** | Connect compound words | well-being, user-friendly |
| **En dash ( – )** | Ranges or connections (with spaces) | June – August, New York – London |
| **Em dash (—)** | Sharp break or abrupt change (no spaces) | I saw him yesterday—he looked great |

## Words and Phrases to Avoid

### Structural Patterns

| Pattern | Example | Fix |
|---------|---------|-----|
| Question sentences | "The result? Improved conversions." | "This led to improved conversions." |
| X/Y juxtapositions | "It's not just about features, it's about benefits." | "Features matter less than benefits." |
| "Stop X. Start Y." | "Stop guessing. Start knowing." | Rewrite into one measurable statement |
| Negation-reveal fragments | "The gap isn't talent. It's action." | Sounds deep, proves nothing. Collapse into one specific claim: "Most people know the playbook. Under 5% ship it in week one." |
| Copula avoidance | "Notion serves as a testament to flexible workflows." | Let it "be": "Notion is flexible." (also: "stands as," "functions as," "boasts," "represents") |
| "-ing" significance tails | "They launched a free tier, highlighting their commitment to accessibility." | End at the fact, or state the real result. (also: "underscoring," "reflecting," "cementing," "ensuring") |
| Rule-of-three padding | "Fast, powerful, and intuitive." | Break the count: use one, two, or four. Flag when it repeats, not on one appearance |
| Formal transition openers | "Furthermore, this improves retention." | "It also improves retention," or start with the substance. (also: "Moreover," "Additionally," "Notably," "Consequently") |
| Hedge preambles | "It's worth noting that pricing affects conversion." | "Pricing affects conversion." (also: "It's important to note that," "One might argue that") |
| Phantom authority | "Studies show onboarding drives retention." | Name the study, or drop the false authority and use a real number. (also: "experts say," "research suggests") |
| Signposted conclusions | "In conclusion, the future is bright." | End on your last real point. No wind-down. (also: "In summary," "Ultimately," "the possibilities are endless") |
| Stakes inflation | "This template marks a new era of productivity." | State the concrete effect: "This template saves about an hour a week." (also: "an indelible mark," "a pivotal moment," "reshaping the industry") |
| Title Case headings | "How To Improve Your Conversion Rate" | Sentence case: "How to improve your conversion rate" |
| Em-dash overuse | "We built it—and it works" | Use periods for separate sentences |

### Generic Business Jargon

Avoid: "leverage," "utilize," "synergy," "game-changer," "paradigm shift"

### AI Vocabulary (content words)

The lexical core of AI-detection lists. These are the single words models reach for far more than humans do. "Delve" is the most-cited tell in the field.

Avoid: "delve," "tapestry," "landscape" (as metaphor), "realm," "crucial," "pivotal," "intricate," "meticulous / meticulously," "vibrant," "showcase," "garner," "foster / fostering," "underscore," "bolster," "boasts," "harness," "unlock," "elevate," "embark," "navigate" (metaphorical)

Fix: plain words. "delve into" → "look at." "the martech landscape" → "the 40-odd tools most SaaS teams run." "harness" → "use." "crucial" → "important" (or cut it).

### Intro Phrases

Avoid: "picture this," "in the realm of," "in the world of"

### False Urgency

Avoid: "you need to," "you must," "essential"

### Vague Qualifiers

Avoid: "very," "really," "quite," "actually"

### Cliche Transitions

Avoid: "at the end of the day," "when all is said and done"

### Command Phrasing (Always AI-like)

Avoid: "Remember," "Keep in mind," "Don't forget"

### Filler Openers

Avoid: "It's time to...," "Let's dive in," "The future of X is here"

## Buzzword Replacement Guide

| Instead of... | Write... |
|---------------|----------|
| "In today's digital landscape" | State the concrete context or feature |
| "Game-changer" / "Next-gen" | Show the change numerically or specifically |
| "Whether you're a beginner or a pro" | Split paths for each audience |
| "X made easy" | Explain how it's easier ("Complete in under 10 minutes") |
| "Transform your workflow" | "Cut steps from 5 to 2; lead time drops 38%" |
| "Seamless," "robust," "intuitive" | Describe what makes them so |
| "World-class" | Show the metric or example |

## How to Sound Genuinely Human

### Use Contractions
"I'm," "you're," "doesn't," "can't"

### Include Occasional Asides
(like this) or [a quick note]

### Reference Specific Dates/Times
"Last Tuesday" not "Recently"

### Mention Places by Name
"When I was working from that coffee shop in Victoria Island" not "When I was working remotely"

### Include Imperfections
Admit uncertainty when appropriate:
- "I'm not sure"
- "It depends"
- "In my case..."

### Vary Sentence Structure
Mix simple statements with more complex thoughts

### Use Cultural References
Books, shows, or events your audience would recognize

### Share Genuine Reactions
"This absolutely floored me" or "I couldn't believe how simple the solution was"

### Use Active, Concrete Verbs
Show what's happening, not just what's being discussed

### Prefer Specifics Over Abstractions
Name tools, data, or people when relevant

### Replace Buzzwords with Real Proof
Metrics, examples, or screenshots

### Be Inclusive and Bias-Free
Use "team" or "everyone" instead of gendered terms

### End Sections with Impact
Leave the reader with an insight or action, not a fade

### Vary Sentence Length Deliberately (Burstiness)
Detectors measure variance in sentence length. AI output is smooth and uniform; humans spike. Alternate a 25-word sentence with a 4-word one. This targets the statistical signal, not just vocabulary, so it's the most durable anti-detection habit.

**Before:** "The tool integrates with your stack, and it automates reporting, and it saves your team several hours each week."
**After:** "The tool plugs into your stack. It automates reporting. That saves the team hours."

### Use Plain Copulas
Let things "be" instead of "serving as" or "standing as."

**Before:** "Onboarding serves as the foundation of retention."
**After:** "Onboarding is where retention starts."

### Repeat a Word Instead of Synonym-Cycling
AI avoids repeating a noun and reaches for a synonym every time ("the platform... the solution... the offering... the system"). Humans just say "the tool" four times. Deliberate repetition reads as human.

**Before:** "The platform is fast. The solution scales well. The offering integrates easily."
**After:** "The tool is fast. The tool scales. The tool integrates easily."

### Break the Rule of Three on Purpose
AI pads ideas into three-item lists and three-adjective stacks for rhythm. Use one item for punch, two for contrast, or four to break the pattern.

**Before:** "fast, powerful, and intuitive"
**After:** "fast, almost annoyingly so"

### Make Contrasts Earn Their Drama
If you set up an "it's not X, it's Y," name whose, which, and what it cost. "The gap isn't talent, it's action" is true of everything and specific to nothing. Test: if flipping the two halves still sounds true, the line is empty.

**Before:** "The gap isn't talent. It's action."
**After:** "The three founders I coached last quarter all knew the playbook. Only one ran it."

### Commit to an Opinion
AI hedges toward neutral balance and "pros and cons of both." A specific stance, a mild contradiction, or an admitted limitation reads as human because AI avoids committing.

**Before:** "There are pros and cons to both approaches."
**After:** "Tiered pricing wins here. Usage-based only makes sense once you're past product-market fit."

### Sentence-Case Your Headings
Capitalize only the first word and proper nouns. Title Case On Every Word is a formatting fingerprint of AI drafts.

### Stop When the Argument Stops
No summary paragraph, no "in conclusion," no forced uplift, no "-ing" significance tail bolted onto the last sentence. End on your last real point.

## Sentence and Paragraph Structure

### Begin with Strong Statements
Start paragraphs with clear, concise claims

### Create Rhythm
Alternate between short, punchy sentences and more detailed explanations

Short. Punchy. Direct.

Then follow with something that expands on the idea and provides the context your reader needs to fully understand what you're saying.

### One Idea Per Paragraph
Keep paragraphs focused on a single thought

### End with Impact
Conclude sections with insights or provocative thoughts

### Use Natural Transitions
Connect ideas organically rather than with formulaic phrases

### Embrace White Space
Short paragraphs create visual breathing room

## Human Rewrite Checklist

Apply these rules in order:

1. **De-AI the diction** - Remove banned phrases, kill false urgency
2. **Add contractions** - Plus natural "I/you" cadence
3. **Use active voice** - Unless there's a deliberate reason not to
4. **Ground it** - Insert a real time/place anchor
5. **Lead with result** - Start with the outcome, number, or decision
6. **Create rhythm** - Mix short and longer sentences; one idea per paragraph; white space
7. **Include one aside and one specific example**
8. **Acknowledge uncertainty** - When appropriate
9. **Add cultural references** - If context allows
10. **Follow punctuation rules** - Proper hyphen/en/em dash spacing
11. **Keep sentences under ~24 words**
12. **Ensure inclusivity** - Neutral phrasing throughout

## Quick Reference: AI Detection Signals

Score each 0-5:

| Signal | What to Look For |
|--------|------------------|
| **Jargon/Cliche** | Buzzwords, AI vocabulary (delve, realm, harness), copula avoidance (serves as, boasts), X/Y patterns, cliche transitions |
| **Dash & Punctuation** | Em-dash overuse, incorrect spacing, Title Case headings, stray smart quotes/arrows |
| **Hedging/Vagueness** | "Very," "really," hedge preambles ("it's worth noting"), phantom authority ("studies show"), generic claims |
| **Structure/Monotony** | Same sentence length (low burstiness), rule-of-three padding, "-ing" tails, formal transitions, signposted conclusions, no white space |
| **Missing Humanity** | No contractions, no specifics, no asides, no committed opinion |
| **Command Phrasing** | "Remember," "Keep in mind," "Don't forget" |

**Weighting caveat:** No single tell is proof. These models learned the patterns from human editorial prose, so rule-of-three, stakes inflation, and AI vocabulary all appear in good human writing. Flag them when they cluster or repeat, and weigh the stack rather than one line. This is a cut-list, not an accusation engine.
