# Measurement Example — Notion

A complete measurement framework worked end to end for Notion, following the Product Marketing Measurement Framework. Notion is a global all-in-one workspace (docs, wikis, projects, and AI) that grows through product-led, bottoms-up adoption. Freemium seeds usage, templates and community pull in new users, and teams expand seat by seat until an enterprise motion takes over. The measurement system has to serve both engines at once, because the same individual who activates on a free workspace is the wedge that later becomes a paid team and then an enterprise account.

Numbers marked _illustrative_ are placeholders for internal figures that are not public. Widely known public facts (Notion's large global user base, its freemium-plus-per-seat model) are used with a light "known" note. No precise internal figures are invented.

---

## 0. Foundations

### Business objective to PMM objective mapping

| Company objective | PMM objective | Example metric |
|---|---|---|
| Grow self-serve revenue | Convert free users into paid seats with clear value and upgrade triggers | Free-to-paid rate, paid-seat conversion |
| Land and expand enterprise | Establish the all-in-one workspace category and arm sales for the top-down motion | Product-qualified accounts, influenced enterprise pipeline |
| Win the category | Own "one workspace instead of five point tools" against Coda, Confluence, Google Docs, ClickUp | Aided recall, message playback, competitive win rate |
| Monetize AI | Drive Notion AI attach on the eligible base | AI attach rate, AI-influenced upgrades |
| Retain and deepen usage | Move users to habitual, collaborative workspace behavior | Weekly active workspaces, feature adoption |

### North Star

**Weekly Collaborating Workspaces (WCW).** Unique workspaces in which two or more members took a meaningful collaborative action in the week: co-editing a doc, working in a shared database, commenting, or assigning work.

Why this north star: it proxies delivered value at the level where Notion actually wins, which is a team creating and collaborating in one shared space rather than a lone user taking notes. Weekly active users is the strong alternative and the honest habit signal, but a raw WAU count rewards solo usage that rarely converts or expands. Collaboration is the behavior that predicts both retention and seat expansion, so it leads revenue instead of lagging it, and it is hard to inflate without real teamwork happening. WAU stays in the tree as a top-line input beneath WCW.

### Metric tree

```
Weekly Collaborating Workspaces (WCW)
 ├─ Reach into segment ── template/SEO traffic, community, referrals, signups
 ├─ Activation depth ──── first doc/database created, first collaborator invited, aha of a shared workspace
 └─ Expansion + commerce ─ seat growth, free-to-paid, Notion AI attach, team-to-enterprise
```

### Guardrails

- Grow signups without letting activation rate fall (acquisition-quality guardrail).
- Grow seat count without raising per-seat churn (expansion-durability guardrail).
- Push Notion AI attach without cannibalizing plan upgrades or spiking refunds (monetization guardrail).
- Increase invite prompts without degrading the first-run experience (activation-friction guardrail).

## 2. PMM-Outcome Metrics (filled)

### Positioning effectiveness

- Do buyers describe Notion as the all-in-one workspace that replaces several point tools, unprompted? Track message playback in reviews, sales calls, and social.
- Category association: Notion filed as a connected workspace, not as a standalone notes app or a standalone project tool.
- Segment conversion lift after a positioning shift (for example, when leaning into wikis or AI).

### Messaging resonance

- Homepage and use-case-page A/B lift on headlines and value props.
- Message tests on the free-versus-paid and AI add-on surfaces.
- Which framings show up in won enterprise deals and in high-intent reviews.
- Sales adoption: are reps using the all-in-one narrative in the enterprise motion?

### Launch performance

- Per launch (Notion AI, Notion Calendar, new features), a scorecard across awareness, engagement, adoption against the eligible base, and revenue.
- Adoption velocity: days from launch to a target adoption threshold.
- Time to first value for the new capability (for AI, the first useful generation inside a real workspace).

### Sales enablement adoption

- For the enterprise motion, are battle cards, one-pagers, and the security and admin narrative driving conversion on calls?
- Content usage rate across the sales team and its correlation with won deals.
- New-rep ramp time using enablement assets.

### Competitive win rate

- Head-to-head win rate against Coda, Confluence, Google Docs, ClickUp, and (for the personal-knowledge segment) Obsidian.
- Loss reasons tagged and trended (price, admin controls, migration cost, feature depth, familiarity of incumbents).
- Displacement rate when Notion replaces a stack of point tools.

### Pricing and packaging

- Tier mix and shift across Free, Plus, Business, and Enterprise.
- Notion AI add-on attach rate and its effect on plan upgrades.
- Discount depth in the enterprise motion versus realized price.
- Willingness to pay signals from research against realized per-seat price.

### Content and demand influence

- Sourced: which templates, SEO pages, or community content were first touch for paid workspaces.
- Influenced: content and product surfaces touched on the path to a paid or enterprise conversion.
- Self-reported: "how did you hear about us" on enterprise inquiries, since much of Notion's pull is dark social and word of mouth inside teams.

## 3. Leading vs Lagging Map (Notion)

| PMM lever | Leading indicators | Lagging outcomes |
|---|---|---|
| Positioning | Message playback, aided recall against point tools | Category win rate, enterprise segment conversion |
| Messaging | Homepage and use-case test lift | Signup-to-paid rate, revenue |
| Launch | Launch-day engagement, adoption velocity (AI, Calendar) | Feature revenue, retention of new adopters |
| Enablement | Battle-card and one-pager usage, rep confidence | Enterprise win rate, ramp time |
| Competitive | Loss-reason trend, displacement rate | Head-to-head win rate |
| Lifecycle | Activation rate, time to value, invite rate | Net revenue retention, seat expansion, churn |
| Monetization | AI attach on eligible base | AI revenue, upgrade rate |

## 4. Attribution and Influence (Notion)

- **Sourced.** Track first-touch surface (template, SEO page, referral invite, paid) for each workspace that converts to paid.
- **Influenced.** Most Notion revenue is influence-heavy and product-led. A user activates on a free workspace, invites a team, and the account converts and expands over months. Last-click will credit the final upgrade screen and miss the template, the collaborator invite, and the habit that did the real work. Weighted multi-touch across product and marketing surfaces is closer to the truth.
- **Product-qualified leads and accounts.** In a PLG-plus-sales model, the highest-signal input to the enterprise motion is not a form fill, it is product usage. Score workspaces on seats, collaboration depth, and admin behavior to surface product-qualified accounts, then measure influenced enterprise pipeline against them.
- **Self-reported.** Lean on "how did you hear about us" for enterprise inquiries, since much of Notion's growth is dark social, sharing inside teams, and word of mouth that trackers cannot see.
- **Portfolio halo.** Judge the free tier, the template gallery, and community content at the portfolio level. They rarely convert directly, but they seed the activated workspaces that later become paid teams and enterprise accounts. Do not kill a top-of-funnel surface on its direct return alone.

### Reporting rituals

- **Weekly pulse:** WCW, WAU, activation rate, time to value, invite rate, any live launch's leading indicators.
- **Monthly review:** free-to-paid rate, seat expansion, Notion AI attach, product-qualified accounts, full register against target.
- **Launch retro:** per launch (Notion AI, Calendar, new features), 2 to 4 weeks after launch, the launch scorecard and time to first value.
- **Quarterly review:** net revenue retention, competitive win rate, influenced enterprise pipeline, the influence story at portfolio level.

### Executive translation (for leadership and the board)

| Notion metric | Business language |
|---|---|
| Weekly Collaborating Workspaces | The count of teams actually working together, which is what retains and expands |
| Activation rate and time to value | More new workspaces reaching value, so lower early churn and a bigger paid funnel |
| Free-to-paid and seat expansion | The self-serve engine turning free usage into revenue that compounds |
| Notion AI attach rate | New monetization layered onto the existing base without new acquisition cost |
| Net revenue retention | Growth that compounds inside accounts, the durable-growth line above 100 percent |
| Influenced enterprise pipeline | Product-led usage feeding the top-down sales motion |

## 6. Anti-patterns to avoid (Notion)

- Reporting total signups or raw WAU as success while collaboration and activation quietly stall. Solo usage rarely converts or expands.
- Crediting the final upgrade screen for a paid conversion that a template, an invite, and months of habit warmed for.
- Chasing template and SEO reach outside the target segment that inflates signups and drags activation down.
- Pushing Notion AI attach in a way that cannibalizes plan upgrades or lifts refunds, a local win that becomes a monetization loss.
- Discounting enterprise deals to hit a quarter and eroding per-seat price integrity across the base.
