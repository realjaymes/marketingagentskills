# B2B ICP Template

In business, ICP stands for **Ideal Customer Profile**—a detailed description of the type of company/account that is most likely to benefit from your product or service and become a valuable customer.

## Example: Growth Marketing Agency ICP

### FIRMOGRAPHICS

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Company Size** | Small to mid-sized businesses / SMB: 11-500 employees (no less than 10 employees, or more than 500+ employees) |
| **Company Industry/Vertical** | B2B, SaaS, Technology, Information and Internet |
| **Market/Location** | US, UK, Canada |
| **Revenue** | $35-50M annual revenue, $100,000 ACV |
| **Technology/Stack** | A/B Testing Tools (e.g. VWO, Optimizely, Convert, Adobe Target), Analytics & Attribution Tools (e.g. GA4), CRM systems (e.g. Salesforce, HubSpot), Marketing Automation (MAP) (e.g. Marketo, HubSpot, Pardot, Eloqua) |

### BUYING COMMITTEE

#### Champions

These individuals drive initial product/vendor research, attend discovery calls, create comparison reports, and advocate internally.

**Relevant Roles:**
- **Director/VP of Growth or Demand Gen** – Concerned with website optimization, lead scoring/routing, and analytics implementation.
- **Marketing Operations Manager** – Looks for better attribution, lead routing, and automation.
- **Growth Marketer** – Focused on increasing conversion rates and pipeline efficiency.
- **Performance Marketing Manager** – Wants to optimize paid media spend and increase conversions.
- **RevOps Manager** – Needs seamless marketing-to-sales handoffs and closed-loop measurement.
- **CRO Specialist** – Seeks conversion rate optimization through experimentation.

**Example Scenario:**
*A Director of Demand Gen realizes that paid acquisition efforts aren't converting efficiently. They research the approach, attend a discovery call, and present the findings to leadership.*

#### Decision-makers

These stakeholders approve budgets, sign contracts, and ensure the solution aligns with company-wide objectives.

**Relevant Roles:**
- **Chief Marketing Officer (CMO)** – Owns revenue growth and overall marketing efficiency.
- **Chief Revenue Officer (CRO)** – Ensures revenue outcomes from marketing efforts.
- **VP of Marketing/Demand Gen** – Oversees acquisition strategy and ROI of marketing investments.
- **Head of Growth** – Evaluates the effectiveness of conversion strategies.

**Example Scenario:**
*A CMO signs off on the engagement after reviewing ROI projections presented by the VP of Growth and Marketing Operations Manager.*

#### Influencers

They don't participate in direct research or negotiations but their opinions shape vendor selection.

**Relevant Roles:**
- **Head of Sales / Sales Ops** – Ensures marketing efforts align with sales pipeline efficiency.
- **Head of Customer Experience (CX)** – Evaluates if optimization aligns with customer journey improvements.
- **Product Marketing Manager** – Ensures website messaging and experiments support product positioning.
- **Head of Analytics / Data Science** – Validates measurement methodology.

#### Blockers

They might oppose due to competing interests, fear of replacement, or skepticism about the value.

**Potential Blockers:**
- **In-House Web Development Team** – May see website optimization services as encroaching on their responsibilities.
- **External Agencies (Performance Marketing, Web Design, CRO, RevOps)** – Agencies currently managing CRO, marketing ops, or analytics may feel threatened.
- **Finance/Procurement** – Might push back on cost concerns if ROI is not clear.
- **Traditional IT / Data Engineering** – Might resist additional tracking scripts or integrations if they see it as a burden.

**Example Scenario:**
*An external CRO agency managing experiments for the company warns the VP of Marketing against switching, arguing that they can already provide similar optimization.*

### ACCOUNT QUALIFICATION

#### A. What criteria and signals make an account a good fit?

- ACV (average contract value) is above $50k
- Actively hiring data analysts or engineers
- Have a specific keyword in their title or About section on LinkedIn (e.g. mobile SDK)
- Have a PLG motion
- Sends qualified leads to an inside sales team
- Spending a lot on paid acquisition channels

#### B. What makes the account a bad fit even if it fits firmographic criteria?

- CFO is in the 1st year in a current position
- Massive layoffs in the past 6 months
- No funding in the past 12-24 months
